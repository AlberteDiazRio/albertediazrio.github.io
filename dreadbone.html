DESCRIPTION

Dreadbone is a real-time strategy and puzzle game where you’re no hero — you’re the one pulling the strings from the shadows. Players shape the terrain, trap enemies with roots, and control ancient relics to protect a fragile explorer stranded on a cursed island. One wrong move, and he’s gone. Precision and timing are everything.

The game blends tactical decision-making with environmental manipulation mechanics, encouraging players to think ahead and strategically manage threats in real time. By combining terrain control, enemy behavior patterns, and relic-based abilities, Dreadbone creates a tense and calculated gameplay experience where every action has consequences.

My role in the project was Gameplay Programmer. I was responsible for implementing core player mechanics, enemy behaviors, and gameplay systems that defined the interaction between terrain manipulation and combat strategy.

I also implemented visual effects (VFX) to reinforce gameplay feedback and improve clarity during combat scenarios. Additionally, I integrated FMOD for audio implementation and was responsible for the full musical implementation of the project.
