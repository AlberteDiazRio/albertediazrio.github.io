About Me

Hello! I’m Alberte Díaz, Game Developer based in Spain. I build projects in Unity and Unreal.

What drives me the most is the feeling of transforming an idea into something playable. I love the moment when mechanics start to “click” and the experience becomes fun.

I enjoy experimenting with mechanics, mixing genres, and exploring how small design decisions impact the overall experience.

Beyond programming and design, I sometimes compose simple music tracks and experiment with 3D art to better understand the full game creation pipeline.

My first memory with videogames goes back to playing Snake and Tetris on my mother’s phone when I was only 5 years old. Even though it was a simple game, it awakened something in me — a fascination with how such small systems could create engaging experiences.

Over the years, that small spark grew into a genuine passion for game development. What started as curiosity eventually turned into a desire to build my own worlds and mechanics.

I’ve been told many times that videogames are useless and that pursuing this path is too difficult. I don’t care. If something truly makes me happy, I will fight for it regardless of how hard it is or other people’s opinions.
